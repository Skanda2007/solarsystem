Our Star, The Sun

Our star is the Sun. It is the source of heat and light which is produced during Nuclear Fusion. The Sun is a Yellow dwarf star or scintifically known as G dwarf star as it is smaller than other stars yet it is so big that it takes about 99.8% of the solar system's mass. It can also fit 1,000,000(1 million) earths in it. The Sun is about 109 times the diameter of Earth which is about 864,000 miles or 1,390,473.22 kilometers. The Sun takes 36 Earth days to complete one rotation or we can say that one day on the sun is around 31.5 days on earth. The Sun's mass is 1.989 x 10 ^ 30 kgs. The temprature of the sun's surface is 1000 f on average. At times the Sun blasts charged paritcles, these streams are known as solar winds . The Sun forms a envelope called heliopause or heliosphere which protects us from the interstellar radiation and is about 1.1247653 x 10^10 miles in radius. Interstellar is the space outside the heliopause. 5 spacecrafts were launched to go to the interstellar by NASA. These 5 are

  1. Voyager 1
  2. Voyager 2
  3. Pioneer 10
  4. Pioneer 11
  5. New Horizons
Out of these Voyager 1, 2 and Pioneer 10 have crossed to Interstellar while the other 2 are still on their way towards the interstellar space.
